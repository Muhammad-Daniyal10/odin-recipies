Pasta

Description

This classic Roman dish is the epitome of simple, ingredient-focused cooking. With just a handful of staple ingredients, it transforms into a rich, creamy, and deeply savory meal. The magic happens when the starchy pasta water emulsifies with the beaten eggs and grated cheese, creating a luxurious sauce without a single drop of heavy cream. It is a perfect, quick meal that highlights how a few quality components can create something extraordinary.

  • 400g dired spaghetti
  • 4 large fresh eggs
  • 150g meat
  • 100g cheese
  • 50g parmesan cheese (freshly grated)
  • black pepper
  • salt
  1. Bring a large pot of heavily salted water to a rolling boil. Add the spaghetti and cook until just shy of al dente (usually about 1 minute less than the package instructions).
  2. While the pasta is cooking, crack the eggs into a medium bowl. Add the grated Pecorino Romano and Parmesan cheese, along with a generous amount of freshly cracked black pepper. Whisk the mixture together until it forms a thick paste.
  3. Place a large skillet over medium heat and add the diced guanciale or pancetta. Cook until the fat has rendered and the meat is crispy and golden brown. Remove the skillet from the heat to ensure the pan isn't too hot for the next step.
  4. Using tongs, transfer the cooked spaghetti directly from the water into the skillet with the crispy meat and its rendered fat. Toss well to coat the pasta evenly.
  5. Quickly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the pasta. Toss vigorously and continuously, adding a small splash of hot pasta water as needed to thin the sauce. The residual heat from the pasta and pan will gently cook the eggs into a silky cream without scrambling them.
  6. Serve immediately, garnished with an extra grating of cheese and a final twist of black pepper.
